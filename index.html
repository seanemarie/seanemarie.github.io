Low Light Plants

Low light plants will grow even in dimly lit rooms.
Snake Plant

Snake Plant (aka Mother-In-Law's Tongue)

Slow growing, low maintenance plant. The only way growers can cause this plant serioes problems is by overwatering it or allowing it to reside in very cold temperatures for long periods.

Care Instructions:

  • Temperature: Average warmth, 60-75°F, no lower than 50°F.
  • Light: copes with low light, indirect or even direct bright light. Thrives in bright light with intermittant sun & shade.
  • Watering: this plant is a succulent, so water in spring-fall when soil becomes dry, and only once per month during winter.
  • Soil: common well draining cactus and succulent potting mix.
  • Re-potting: does not enjoy re-potting. Re-pot only when the pot cracks from growth in a clay pot.
Cast Iron Plant

Cast Iron Plant

The cast iron plan recieves its common name for its ability to withstand neglect. Easy to care for foliage that has a tolerant attitude to light, dry air, and lack of watering.

Care Instructions:

  • Temperature: Average warmth of 60-75°F, with ideal temperature above 45°F.
  • Light: low light or bright indirect light. Direct light will damage leaves,
  • Watering: water once top inch of soil is dry, and even less often during the winter. Do not over water.
  • Repot: only when needed (in Spring) or once every 5 years.
  • Soil: well-draining potting mix with good drainage holes at the bottom of the pot.

Medium Indirect Light Plants

Indirect sunlight plants thrive in bright rooms with indirect sun.
ZZ Plant

ZZ plant (aka Zamioculcas Zamifolia)

The ZZ plant is an easy-to-grow and care for indoor plant, that grows up to 3 feet. Light requirements are flexible (low light is okay, but indirect is ideal).

Care Instructions:

  • Temperature: Average warmth of 60-75°F, with ideal temperature above 45°F.
  • Light: Bright indirect light is ideal, although it will tolerate low light.
  • Watering: allow soil to become dry-to-the-touch at the top between watering. Best to water less than too much.
  • Repot: once a year at the beginning of spring. Good drainage holes at bottom of pot is essential.
  • Soil: well-draining potting mix is ideal.
Christmas Cactus

Christmas Cactus

Hardy little plant that is tricky to grow. The secret is to realize it is a tropical cactus, and not at all like its desert dwelling cousins, requiring much more watering and careful attention. Gets its name because it typically blooms around Christmas and Easter.

Care Instructions:

  • Temperature: Best kept at 65°F, except during February-March which is its "resting period". At this time the cactus should be kept at 55°F. Plant death can occur below 50°F.
  • Light: late fall and all winter, direct sunlight is preferred. Any other time of year plants should be grown in partial sun conditions.
  • Top inch of soil should be kept moist to the touch at all times. Never permit soil to become water logged or stand in saucer. Normal air humidity is okay, with frequent misting.
  • Repot: repotting is required every 3 years in late spring.
  • Soil: 60% potting soil, 40% bark or perlite.
